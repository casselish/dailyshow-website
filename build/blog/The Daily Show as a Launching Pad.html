Title

The Daily Show as a Launching Pad

Date

November 4, 2020

Time

6:20pm

Content

Over the years, The Daily Show became a launching pad for aspiring comedians and satirists alike. Stephen Colbert worked for the show before Jon Stewart arrived, but a character he developed -- one of a conservative pundit, modeled after Bill O’Reilly -- on the show, as well as his general success as a correspondent under Jon Stewart, led him to his own network deal with The Colbert Report. Similarly, Samantha Bee, also a former correspondent, earned her show as a result of being a correspondent; Hasan Minhaj and John Oliver did, as well.

Moreover, even if a comedian never worked for the Daily Show, the increase in satirical news shows over the last decade can surely be attributed to Stewart. The content of SNL’s “Weekend Update,” for example, has become slightly more cutting, and more traditional late night hosts like Seth Meyers have also started dabbling in satire-themed segments. In many ways, Stewart kickstarted the satire-filled television landscape we now have.

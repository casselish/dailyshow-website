Title

How I Was Introduced to the Daily Show

Date

November 4, 2020

Time

9:45am

Content

You may be wondering: why have I decided to create an entire blog based on the history of The Daily Show? If so, you’ve come to the right place. I think it’s safe to say that I’ve enjoyed satire since I was a kid, but, for a long time, my primary satirical news source was the now-defunct Colbert Report, wherein Stephen Colbert played a conservative pundit to great effect. By comparison, the Daily Show always seemed a bit boring to me -- after all, Jon Stewart wasn’t even playing a character, and what was the fun in that?

However, once I bought and read (and read and read and read) the oral history of the Daily Show, I started to appreciate just how much it changed television’s news landscape. Satire on cable television had always seemed a faraway, somewhat silly concept before Jon Stewart went and made it one of the most popular shows on Comedy Central. Looking at what the Daily Show covered produce an accurate rendition of what the left was feeling during certain events. The Daily Show should not be underestimated as a historical record.
